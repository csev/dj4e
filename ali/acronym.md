Your idea is closely related to a SPOC, but it reverses the usual direction.

SPOC means Small Private Online Course. It usually takes MOOC-style online materials and uses them with a limited, selected group—often registered university students—with greater instructor interaction, discussion, and support. Harvard’s definition emphasizes limited enrollment and materials that are not necessarily open to everyone.

The key relationship

A traditional SPOC often works like this:

Bring online-course materials into a private campus class.

Your model works more like this:

Bring outside online learners into the instructional orbit of a live campus class.

That is a meaningful difference.

Similarities

Both models reject the anonymous, self-paced, massive character commonly associated with MOOCs.

Both offer:

a defined cohort or schedule;
meaningful instructor or TA access;
live interaction;
more structure and support;
a stronger connection between online learning and university teaching.

SPOCs are frequently associated with blended or flipped learning and greater personal interaction than MOOCs.

Differences

The defining variables are different:

Model	Defining characteristic
MOOC	Massive and open
SPOC	Small and private
CAOC	Aligned with a campus course
BOOC	Open, but blended with a live instructional environment

A SPOC says who may enter and how large the group is. A CAOC says what the online course is aligned with.

Therefore, they are not mutually exclusive. A course could be:

a SPOC but not a CAOC: a private online professional-development course with no campus counterpart;
a CAOC but not a SPOC: openly available online learners following a campus schedule;
both: a capped, selected online cohort aligned with a campus class.
Where DJ4E appears to fit

Based on your description, DJ4E is probably not naturally a SPOC unless you intend to restrict admission or cap enrollment.

It sounds more like:

A Campus-Aligned Open Online Course

That suggests CAOOC, although the extra O is visually awkward.

Or:

A Campus-Aligned Online Course with open participation

That gives you CAOC, while “open” remains a property of the particular offering rather than part of the acronym.

The strongest conceptual distinction may be:

A SPOC brings a MOOC into the campus classroom. A CAOC connects online learners outward to the campus classroom.

That sentence makes CAOC feel like a genuinely useful new category rather than merely another synonym for SPOC.
