AI in Programming Classes: Charles Severance's Approach

programming education, AI tools, curriculum design, zone of proximal development, cumulative exams, auto graders, software development

Charles Severance, a professor at the University of Michigan School of Information, discusses the integration of AI in programming education. He argues that AI is now an essential part of software development and must be used by students to become better programmers. Severance, who teaches large courses such as Python for Everybody (with three million students) and Django for Everybody, emphasizes the importance of programming fundamentals over AI dependency.

His approach includes designing assignments that require synthesizing multiple course concepts, using cumulative exams to discourage AI reliance in early weeks, and auto graders that check for specific techniques. Severance observes that AI can handle initial assignments but fails at the "knee of the curve" (around week six), where the complexity increases. This strategy ensures students build foundational skills before using AI effectively.
